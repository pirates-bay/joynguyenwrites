About

A journalist who writes about travel the way locals live it

I was born and raised in central Vietnam. Travel entered my life early because my parents loved taking our family on short trips around the country: Ho Chi Minh City, Nha Trang, Da Lat, Hanoi, and the places between them. Those trips taught me that a destination is not only a landmark; it is food, family rhythm, language, bargaining, weather, roads, and the small kindnesses that make a place feel human. That conviction still shapes every story I file.

During university in Vietnam, I traveled the country with friends on old Honda motorbikes and spent three months in Cambodia on an internship with Viettel Post, working my way from Phnom Penh to Angkor Wat and Koh Rong. After my family moved to the United States, I built my English through writing courses, competitions, and certificates — then spent two years in a corporate job before admitting that a desk was never going to be my beat.

I resigned after a promotion, flew one-way to Lima, and gave myself six months on the road. Peru was meant to be three weeks; it became the second country I know deeply. I crossed it overland — Lima, Paracas, Huacachina, Arequipa, Puno, Cusco — then kept going: Brazil for Christ the Redeemer, Bolivia for the Uyuni salt flats, Italy, Jordan, India, China, chasing the Seven Wonders and the stories between them.

Joy Nguyen Writes is where that career comes together: independent travel journalism and research on Peru and Vietnam, published with named sources, visible review dates, and no advertising, sponsorships, or affiliate links.

Career

Three countries, one beat: how people move through a place

  1. Vietnam

    Roots and first fieldwork

    Raised in central Vietnam and educated there, Joy learned the country from the ground: family road trips, university motorbike journeys through the highlands and coast, and a three-month posting in Cambodia with Viettel Post. Vietnam remains the place she knows from the inside — and the subject of half her published work.

  2. United States

    The writing years

    After emigrating with her family, Joy trained as a writer the hard way: language courses, writing competitions, professional certificates, and two years inside American corporate life. The discipline stuck; the desk didn't.

  3. Peru & beyond

    Independent journalism

    A one-way ticket to Lima began six months of field reporting across South America and onward through more than twenty countries. Peru became her second area of expertise — its buses, routes, operators, and the gap between what travelers are promised and what they get. Today she publishes Joy Nguyen Writes and its sister guide, Day Trips Vietnam.

Method

How every story gets reported

  1. Start with the traveler's problem. Every piece begins with a real planning question: route, budget, safety, timing, transport, or experience.
  2. Test it against the ground. Claims are checked against lived experience in Vietnam and Peru, recurring traveler reports, and current conditions.
  3. Separate facts from judgment. Dates, routes, prices, and official rules are sourced and linked; recommendations explain their reasoning.
  4. Date it, and revisit it. Every article carries a visible review date, because transport, access, prices, and safety change.

FAQ

Common questions

Who is Joy Nguyen?

Joy Nguyen is a travel journalist and researcher born in central Vietnam. Her career has moved from Vietnam to the United States to Peru, and she publishes independent reporting and research on travel in both countries.

Why is she qualified to cover Peru and Vietnam?

She was born and raised in central Vietnam, traveled the country extensively by motorbike, worked in the United States, and spent six months reporting from Peru and South America. Her work combines that lived experience with published tourism research and named sources.

What does she write about?

Travel in Peru and Vietnam: overland routes, transport safety, tour operators, destination research, food, culture, and practical planning. Her published work includes two research libraries and two operator review series totaling 195 articles.

Does she earn money from bookings or affiliate links?

No. Joy Nguyen Writes carries no advertising, no sponsorships, and no affiliate links. Recommendations are editorially independent, and each article separates first-hand judgment from externally sourced facts.
