Editorial method

How I evaluate travel advice before publishing

  1. Start with the traveler problem. Advice begins with a real planning question: route, budget, safety, timing, transport, or experience.
  2. Compare against on-the-ground patterns. I look for what travelers repeatedly need to know, not only what looks good in a search result.
  3. Separate facts from judgment. Dates, routes, prices, and official rules should be sourced; recommendations should explain their logic.
  4. Update when conditions change. Travel content should carry a visible review date because transport, access, prices, and safety guidance change.
