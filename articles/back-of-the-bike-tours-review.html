Back of the Bike Tours review: what travelers should know before booking

A strong Saigon food-and-motorbike pick for travelers who want controlled adventure rather than a purely walking food tour.

By Joy Nguyen Last reviewed April 24, 2026
Quick answer: A strong Saigon food-and-motorbike pick for travelers who want controlled adventure rather than a purely walking food tour.

This is a review synthesis, not a sponsored recommendation and not a claim of personal use. I read the public review signals travelers normally check before booking: Tripadvisor, Google Maps review panels, and the operator context visible from current public profiles.

Best for

Travelers who want a Saigon motorbike food tour where the ride, food, guide conversation, and traffic experience are all part of the product.

What Tripadvisor shows

Tripadvisor lists Back of the Bike Tours at 5.0 with more than 3,000 reviews, and the profile focuses on Saigon street food by motorbike with local English-speaking guides.

What Google review signals add

Google review signals are useful for checking driver confidence, recent food-stop quality, dietary handling, and whether guests felt safe as passengers.

What travelers seem to praise

  • Reviews repeatedly mention feeling safer than expected on the back of the bike.
  • Food variety and guide conversation appear to be the core strengths.
  • Travelers often value getting outside the most touristy District 1 food pattern.

Watch-outs before you book

  • Confirm whether car support or non-motorbike options exist if someone is nervous.
  • Ask how the company handles rain, helmets, insurance, and dietary limits.
  • Check whether the tour includes alcohol or if drinks are optional.

Joy's verdict

A strong Saigon food-and-motorbike pick for travelers who want controlled adventure rather than a purely walking food tour.

How to use reviews wisely

Do not treat a high rating as the whole decision. Sort Tripadvisor and Google reviews by newest first, then look for repeated patterns around pickup timing, refund handling, guide communication, vehicle quality, food safety, safety briefing, and whether the delivered tour matched the product page. One angry review can be noise; repeated operational complaints are a signal to ask sharper questions before paying.
