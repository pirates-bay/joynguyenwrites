Informal transport risk

Why informal transport is the wrong place to save money in Peru

Do not choose a cheap informal car, van, or bus for intercity travel or tours in Peru.

By Joy Nguyen Last reviewed April 24, 2026
Quick answer: Do not choose a cheap informal car, van, or bus for intercity travel or tours in Peru.

SUTRAN warns that informal vehicles may lack MTC authorization, current technical inspections, and SOAT coverage.

The cheaper ticket can hide the most expensive risk: no proper authorization, unclear insurance, weak vehicle checks, and no reliable accountability if something goes wrong.

What this means for travelers

In Peru, transport is part of the travel experience. Bus terminals, pickup points, tour vehicles, altitude, road conditions, timed tickets, and local access rules can decide whether a day feels smooth or stressful. Treat the transport plan as a core part of the itinerary, not a line item to solve later.

How to use the finding

  • Book with identifiable companies instead of street offers.
  • Avoid unmarked vans outside terminals and tourist zones.
  • Ask whether passenger insurance applies to the actual service.
  • Treat very low prices as a reason to ask more questions.

The strongest Peru bus and tour plans are specific. They name the route, operator type, pickup point, arrival buffer, ticket dependency, and backup option. That level of detail helps travelers avoid both panic and overconfidence.
